About

To my mind, the 8-bit computing technology developed in the 1970s and 1980s represents the genesis of the computer-centric world we live in today. Newly developed computer processors by the likes of MOS, Intel, Zilog, and Motorola ushered in a wave of innovation in the field of computer science. Once relegated to the realms of large corporations and academic institutions, companies by the likes of Apple, Commodore, and Tandy Radio Shack ushered in a new era of computers for the home user- the genesis of the personal computers we all use today. Additionally, these technologies gave birth to the home video game console. The 8-bit consoles of the 1980s made companies such as Atari, Nintendo, and Sega household names.
